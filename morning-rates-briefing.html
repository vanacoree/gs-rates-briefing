🔴 Peripheral Spread Monitor vs. 10Y Bund · basis points · click for source
What is this? A "peripheral spread" is the difference between a country's 10-year government bond yield and Germany's 10-year Bund yield (the eurozone's risk-free benchmark). It measures how much extra yield investors demand to hold that country's debt vs. the safest eurozone borrower. A rising spread = markets see more risk (political instability, fiscal stress, or ECB tightening fears). A falling spread = improving confidence. For example, BTP–Bund at 73bps means Italy's 10Y bond pays 3.73% while Germany pays 3.00% — investors demand 73 basis points of extra compensation to hold Italian debt. During the 2022 eurozone crisis, the BTP–Bund spread spiked to 251bps; today's 73bps reflects dramatically improved Italian fiscal credibility and ECB backstop confidence.
